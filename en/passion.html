My passions

Music

With my sister playing the violin and my brother the flute, I was immersed in music from a young age. To fully dive into it, I decided to play the viola for about 8 to 9 years. The viola is similar to a violin but larger, producing a deeper sound.
I took part in several concerts at the conservatories of Dreux and Évreux in Normandy. I was part of a symphony orchestra at the Dreux conservatory and later joined a jazz group with my sister at the Évreux conservatory.
Playing music taught me discipline, perseverance, and creativity.

Bowling

After stopping the viola, I wanted to find another passion to pursue. I came across a bowling club and immediately got hooked on the sport. I particularly enjoyed the friendly atmosphere and the slightly competitive side of the game. Playing with older people helped me improve my technique as well as my social skills. Through bowling, I gained patience, perseverance, and team spirit.

Video games

People can say a lot about video games, but they also teach many things:

  • Hand-eye coordination
  • Problem solving
  • Teamwork
  • Time management
  • Creativity
  • Perseverance
  • Memory

Anyway, we’re not here to debate that x). I play various games of all kinds. I spend a lot of time on Valorant or Phasmophobia, but I also play other games such as Minecraft (mostly vanilla survival), Borderlands (finished all except the 4th one — still need to finish it), Wolfenstein (also need to finish it), and It Takes Two (finished with my girlfriend).
